AI Masterclass Notes

Complete lecture notes covering Neural Networks, Backpropagation, and Optimizers.

1. The Perceptron (Neuron)

The fundamental building block of a neural network. It computes a weighted sum of inputs plus a bias.

MATH
$$ z = \sum_{i=1}^{n} w_i x_i + b $$ $$ a = \sigma(z) $$
💡 Key Concept:
The Activation Function ($\sigma$) introduces non-linearity. Without it, a deep neural network would be no more powerful than a single linear regression model.

2. Backpropagation

Backpropagation is the algorithm used to train neural networks by calculating the gradient of the Loss Function ($L$) with respect to the weights.

2.1 The Chain Rule

To update a weight deep inside the network, we propagate the error backward:

CALCULUS
$$ \frac{\partial L}{\partial w} = \frac{\partial L}{\partial a} \cdot \frac{\partial a}{\partial z} \cdot \frac{\partial z}{\partial w} $$

3. Optimizers

Once we have the gradients, the optimizer updates the weights to minimize loss.

3.1 Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD)

FORMULA
$$ w_{new} = w_{old} - \alpha \frac{\partial L}{\partial w} $$
🚀 Pro Tip:
In your Traffic Sign project, use the Adam optimizer instead of SGD. It converges faster because it adapts the learning rate ($\alpha$) for each weight individually.
